# Changelog

All notable changes to the Clearance Manifest standard (the spec, schemas, JSON-LD context, rule registry, vocabulary, and conformance examples) are recorded here. The format follows [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/en/1.1.0/); versioning is per [`GOVERNANCE.md`](GOVERNANCE.md) and [`VERSIONING.md`](VERSIONING.md) (semver at the spec level). Published versioned URLs are permanent and immutable per `VERSIONING.md`.

Artifacts are served at `https://openclearance.org/v0.1/...`.

## [v0.1.0] - 2026-06-07

Initial public release of the Clearance Manifest standard.

- **Conceptual model: payload and envelope that never mix.** A Clearance Manifest is a portable, fail-closed, machine-readable JSON-LD document asserting whether one specific creative work is rights-cleared for reuse. The pure JSON-LD **payload** describes the work, its source, the rights determination, the clearance booleans, the determination event, and citations. A thin **envelope** wraps the payload to carry integrity (and, in higher tiers, authenticity). The envelope references the payload; the payload never references the envelope, never contains its own hash or signature, and never carries commercial data.
- **A deny is a valid answer.** A manifest is emitted for cleared and non-cleared works alike. Any rights signal that is missing, ambiguous, or not affirmatively permissive resolves to a deny: every clearance boolean `false`, `confidence: "low"`, `statement: null`, and a `basis` (rule `default-deny`) carrying the exact failing value. A bug that lets a non-cleared record present as cleared is a critical defect; a false deny is tolerable.
- **Compose, don't reinvent.** The format binds existing standards through its JSON-LD `@context`: Creative Commons rights URIs (v0.1 emits the CC0 and Public Domain Mark URIs, with the model accommodating rightsstatements.org URIs a future version may admit), schema.org and Dublin Core (descriptive and provenance metadata), and W3C PROV (the determination event). Its only original contribution is the thin clearance layer under the `oc:` vocabulary.
- **Byte-exact Tier-0 integrity envelope.** The keyless Tier-0 envelope carries the manifest as its **exact UTF-8 JSON string** and the integrity hash is the SHA-256 of those exact bytes, as lowercase hex. Shape: `{ tier: 0, payloadType: "application/clearance-manifest+json", payload: "<exact JSON string>", integrity: { alg: "sha-256", hash } }`. A consumer hashes the `payload` string's bytes verbatim, compares against `integrity.hash`, and only then parses. This is the shape DSSE, JWS, COSE, and C2PA all use (integrity over bytes, never over a re-parsed object); it is content-addressing-correct and removes the canonicalization attack surface entirely, with no canonicalization library. (An earlier pre-publication draft hashed an RFC 8785 / JCS canonicalization; v0.1 is byte-exact instead.)
- **Three-tier trust model as an adoption funnel.** Tier 0 (hash only: integrity, no authenticity), Tier 1 (an attestor signs on behalf of the named actor, via C2PA), Tier 2 (the actor signs directly via `did:web` / X.509). Actor and signer are deliberately separate: the actor makes the determination, the signer vouches. Verification produces a standardized `VerificationState` (`REJECTED`, `UNVERIFIED_SIGNAL`, `ATTESTED_DELEGATE`, `ATTESTED_DIRECT`). A commercial gate MUST require an attested state; a Tier-0 manifest carries integrity but not authenticity and MUST NOT, on its own, gate a commercial transaction. Tiers 1/2 are defined here but ship no signing code in v0.1.
- **`clearance` facets with structured `basis`.** Binary, machine-actionable facets (`commercialReproduction`, `derivatives`, `attributionRequired`), each paired with a `basis` whose `rule`, `inputs`, and `summary` are all REQUIRED (honesty by architecture). The pattern extends without breaking older consumers.
- **`verification` as an auditable event.** Records how the determination was reached, distinct from how the clearance booleans were derived. `determinedBy.actor` and `role` name who made the call (the rights source on a clear, the engine rights-gate on a deny), and `determinationSource` points at the evidence relied upon.
- **The advisory contract: structural validity is independent of rule recognition.** The set of `basis.rule` ids is open, not a closed enum. A well-formed `rule` not in the registry baseline is still a structurally valid manifest; a conforming verifier MUST emit a non-fatal `unrecognised_rule` advisory and MUST NOT reject the document for it.
- **Machine artifacts (frozen at `/v0.1/`).** JSON Schemas (Draft 2020-12) for the manifest payload (`clearance-manifest.schema.json`), the Tier-0 envelope (`tier0-envelope.schema.json`), and the verifier advisory output (`advisory-entry.schema.json`); the JSON-LD context (`context.jsonld`) as the sole normative authority for term expansion; the non-normative determination-rule registry (`/v0.1/rules`, the CC0/PD truth tables and `default-deny`); and the vocabulary term map (`/v0.1/vocab`). `specVersion` (`"0.1"`) is a human-readable convenience only.
- **Conformance examples and check.** Reference Tier-0 envelopes (one accepted, one deny) under `/v0.1/examples/`, exercised by a dependency-light conformance check (`scripts/conformance.mjs`, `npm run conformance`) that validates every schema and the context, recomputes the byte-exact integrity hash on every example, and validates each parsed payload against the manifest schema. CI runs it on every change to the spec artifacts.
- **Commerce binding, out of core.** Commercial data is never nested in a manifest. It travels as a separate sibling assertion under a vendor namespace that references the manifest by its content hash. The direction is invariant: commerce references clearance, never the reverse.
- **Governance suite.** `GOVERNANCE.md` (decision process, change types, RFC windows, deprecation, the open long-term governance question), `CONTRIBUTING.md`, `VERSIONING.md` (the URL-stability and immutability promise), `SECURITY.md`, `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`, `SUPPORT.md`, and `CITATION.cff`.

The standard is emitted today by the [open-museum-mcp](https://github.com/cfpramod/open-museum-mcp) engine.
